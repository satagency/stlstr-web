## SetLister Revenue Model: How It Works

SetLister is embedded directly into the ticketing flow. When a fan purchases a ticket through Ticketmaster, they are presented with a SetLister voting module — a 4-tier song voting system where fans pay to influence the artist's setlist.

### The Minnesota State Lottery Model

SetLister voting revenue operates on the same principle as a state lottery purchase: **the transaction is final at the moment of payment.** There is no refund. There is no dispute. There is no chargeback path on a vote.

This is not a loophole or an oversight — it is a deliberate design decision rooted in decades of consumer precedent. Every lottery player in America understands that paying for a chance at an outcome is not a guarantee of that outcome. SetLister fans understand the same thing. They are paying for the right to participate, to be heard, to have their preference counted. Whether their song makes the final setlist is part of the experience — not a breach of contract.

**In the history of SetLister, not a single fan has requested a refund on a vote. Zero.**

That is not luck. That is product-market fit. Fans are not confused about what they are buying. They want in.

We say this directly because we know who we are talking to. Ticketmaster and AXS operate at a scale and complexity that few companies in any industry can match. We are not bringing you a liability. We are bringing you a revenue stream with a clean consumer posture — fan-voluntary, lottery-precedent, zero chargeback exposure — and we want to build it together.

## The Merch Credit: How We Keep Every Fan Happy

Every participating artist on SetLister has agreed to the following policy:

> **If a fan's voted song does not appear in the final setlist, the dollar value of their vote automatically converts to a credit toward official artist merchandise.**

A fan who spent $10 on a Rare Vote and didn't hear their song gets $10 off a hoodie. A fan who dropped $25 on a Legendary Vote walks away with a meaningful discount on merch they were probably going to buy anyway.

This is good product design that closes every conceivable loop cleanly:

- Fan is satisfied regardless of setlist outcome
- Artist drives additional merch revenue from fans who might not have otherwise purchased
- Ticketmaster and AXS have zero exposure to complaints routed through their platforms
- Every stakeholder wins

We designed this into the product from day one — not as a reaction to pressure, but because building something fans trust for the long term requires thinking through every scenario in advance. That's the standard we hold ourselves to, and it's the standard we want to bring to this partnership.

## Fan Stickiness: The Strategic Play

Revenue is the short-term story. Fan stickiness is the long-term play for Ticketmaster.

**The Problem Ticketmaster Has Today:**
Fans buy a ticket. They leave Ticketmaster's ecosystem immediately. No re-engagement. No loyalty signal. No data about which fans are most valuable. No reason to stay inside the TM app or platform.

**What SetLister Changes:**

| Moment | Fan Behavior | TM Value |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Ticket purchase | Fan enters SetLister voting flow inside TM checkout | Increased session time, higher avg transaction value |
| Pre-show (days before) | Fan checks vote standings, shares votes on social | Organic TM platform re-engagement without paid media |
| Show night | Fan sees their voted song on the real setlist | Emotional connection reinforced via TM-branded experience |
| Post-show | Fan receives digital setlist collectible with their vote embedded | TM becomes associated with the memory, not just the transaction |
| Next tour announcement | Fan returns to TM first — because their history lives there | **Lifetime value multiplied** |

**The metric Ticketmaster cannot currently measure:**
> "Which fans are superfans, and how do we capture more of their spend?"

SetLister answers this. Every vote tier reveals fan intensity. A fan who pays $25 for a Legendary Vote is a different customer than a fan who pays $3 for a Standard Vote. That data — purchase-intent + fandom intensity, layered onto ticketing history — is the most valuable behavioral data in live music.

**Ticketmaster currently cannot see this. SetLister creates it.**
